About
Aaron Lee
Writer · Savannah, Georgia

I'm a 6th grader from Savannah, Georgia who thinks too hard about everything, plays violin and tennis, and writes it all down.

I started writing in 5th grade — mostly just daily journal entries about games I played and trips I took. Then somewhere along the way the writing got more interesting. I started writing from the point of view of a lonely tennis ball. I wrote about kids mining cobalt in the Congo. I wrote a poem about freedom seven times until I got it right.

I don't write because I have to. I write because it's the best way I know to figure out what I actually think.

A few things about me
  • I play violin — the kind of practice where you do it for hours and it still doesn't sound right, and then one day it does.
  • I play competitive tennis. I went from losing in the first round to winning tournaments. That took about a year of really boring drills.
  • I think about big problems — energy, food waste, cobalt mines. I've been known to invent solutions and then write stories about them.
  • I've been to Korea, Washington D.C., Chattanooga, and a campsite where we ran out of gas on the highway.
  • My room has a 2016 Charizard holo, tennis trophies, a spelling bee trophy, and a rubber duck collection.
